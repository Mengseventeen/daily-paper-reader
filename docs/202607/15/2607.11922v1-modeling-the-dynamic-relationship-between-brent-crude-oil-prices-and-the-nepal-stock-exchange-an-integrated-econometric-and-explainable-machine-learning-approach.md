## 摘要
本研究采用结合传统计量经济学技术与机器学习及可解释人工智能技术的综合方法，探讨全球油价与尼泊尔证券交易所之间的动态关系。为此，我们分析了约十三年（2013年6月至2026年6月）的国际油价和NEPSE指数日度数据，运用格兰杰因果检验、EGARCH(1,1)和DCC-GARCH模型考察了预测关系、非对称波动行为以及时变相关性等不同特性。为进一步补充计量经济学分析，我们采用随机森林、LightGBM和XGBoost等机器学习模型来捕捉非线性关系，并运用SHAP值、部分依赖图和个体条件期望图等可解释人工智能技术来进一步解释模型结果。计量经济学分析结果显示，布伦特原油对NEPSE存在滞后四天的统计显著单向格兰杰因果关系，两个市场均表现出高波动持续性，且条件相关性较弱但高度时变。在机器学习模型中，XGBoost表现最佳；可解释性分析表明，NEPSE自身的动能和短期波动主要影响其自身行为，而石油相关信息作为次要且依赖于方法的贡献因素。研究结果表明，计量经济学与可解释机器学习方法能够以互补的方式揭示石油与股票市场之间的关系。

## Abstract
This study examines the dynamic relationship between the global oil prices and Nepal Stock Exchange (NEPSE) using an integrated approach which combines traditional econometric techniques with machine learning and explainable AI techniques. For this, Daily data of International Oil prices and NEPSE index is analyzed from approximately thirteen years (June 2013 to June 2026) using Granger causality, EGARCH(1,1), and DCC-GARCH models to examine different properties like predictive relationships, asymmetric volatility behaviour, and time-varying correlations. To further supplement the econometric analysis, Machine Learning Models like Random Forest, LightGBM, and XGBoost algorithms were used to capture nonlinear relationships, along with explainable artificial intelligence techniques like SHAP values, Partial Dependence Plots, and Individual Conditional Expectation plots to further interpret the results of the model. The results from the econometric analysis showed a statistically significant unidirectional Granger causality from Brent crude oil to NEPSE with a four-day lag, high volatility persistence in both markets, and weak yet highly time-varying conditional correlations. Among the machine learning models, XGBoost achieves the best performance, and explainability analysis reveals that NEPSE own momentum and short-term volatility mainly influence its own behaviour and oil-related information serves as a minor, method-dependent contributor. The findings demonstrate that econometric and explainable machine learning approaches provide insights into the oil and equity market relationship in a way that each approach complements the result of one another.
