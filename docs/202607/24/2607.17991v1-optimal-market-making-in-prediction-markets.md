## 摘要
随着交易量的上升和实际相关性的增加，预测市场正吸引越来越多的关注。为确保有效的价格发现，流动性提供变得愈发重要。由于预测市场中二元结算结构的存在，最优做市导致了一个与经典设置中研究的问题根本不同的优化问题。在本文中，我们为预测市场开发了一个随机控制框架，其中市场价格被建模为由一个变换后的潜在信念扩散生成的结果的条件概率。做市商选择买价和卖价以最大化期望期末财富，同时控制盯市库存风险和结算时剩余头寸的结算风险。我们推导了相关的Hamilton-Jacobi-Bellman方程，并刻画了唯一的最优买价和卖价。通过将该方程变换到潜在信念空间并使用不动点论证，我们证明了经典解的存在唯一性，并验证了由此得到的最优报价策略。此外，我们提供了数值分析，揭示了预测市场中的最优流动性提供如何依赖于库存、市场信念、距离结算时间和风险厌恶。进一步，我们证明，与最大化瞬时期望盯市利润的短视基准相比，最优报价策略在保留大部分期望利润的同时显著改善了下跌保护。

## Abstract
Prediction markets are attracting growing attention as trading volumes rise and their practical relevance increases. To ensure efficient price discovery, liquidity provision becomes ever more important. Due to the binary settlement structure in prediction markets, optimal market making leads to an optimization problem that is fundamentally different from the ones studied in classical settings. In this paper, we develop a stochastic control framework for prediction markets in which the market price is modeled as a conditional probability of the outcome that is generated by a transformed latent belief diffusion. A market maker selects bid and ask quotes to maximize expected terminal wealth while controlling both mark-to-market inventory risk and the settlement risk of remaining positions at resolution. We derive the associated Hamilton--Jacobi--Bellman equation and characterize the unique optimal bid and ask quotes. By transforming the equation to the latent belief space and using a fixed-point argument, we prove existence and uniqueness of a classical solution and verify the resulting optimal quoting strategy. In addition, we provide a numerical analysis, which reveals how optimal liquidity provision in prediction markets depends on inventory, market beliefs, time to resolution, and risk aversion. Further, we demonstrate that the optimal quoting strategy substantially improves downside protection while preserving most of its expected profit relative to a myopic benchmark that maximizes the instantaneous expected mark-to-market profit.
